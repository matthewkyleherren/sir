**Recipe 1: Classic Swiss Cervelas with Mustard**
Bold, grilled sausage in just 15 minutes. Paired with tangy Swiss mustard and fresh bread.

**the breakdown**
- prep time: 5 min
- cook time: 10 min
- serves: 2
- total: ~15 min

**ingredients:**
- 4 Cervelas sausages
- 2 tbsp Swiss mustard (mild or sharp)
- 4 slices rustic bread
- 1 small onion (thinly sliced, optional)
- Butter (for bread)
- Fresh chives (for garnish)

**Note/Tip:** Score the sausages for extra char. Perfect with a cold Sir Beer lager.

**instructions:**
1. Preheat the grill - Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
2. Prepare ingredients - Slice onion thinly. Butter the bread slices.
3. Score sausages - Make diagonal cuts across each Cervelas.
4. Grill sausages - Place sausages on grill. Cook 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden and slightly charred.
5. Toast bread - Grill buttered bread for 1-2 minutes per side.
6. Plate and garnish - Serve sausages with bread, mustard, and onions. Garnish with chives.
7. Enjoy! - Best paired with a crisp Swiss lager.

**Recipe 2: Swiss Raclette Bites**
A cozy classic with melted cheese and potatoes. Topped with pickles and cured meat for tradition.

**the breakdown**
- prep time: 10 min
- cook time: 20 min
- serves: 2
- total: 30 min

**ingredients:**
- 8 oz Raclette cheese (sliced)
- 4 medium potatoes (fingerling or new)
- 4 cornichons (sliced)
- 4 slices cured meat (Bündnerfleisch or prosciutto)
- 1 small onion (pickled, optional)
- Fresh cracked black pepper
- Olive oil
- Salt to taste

**Note/Tip:** Use a raclette grill for authentic Swiss experience. Pairs beautifully with alpine lager.

**instructions:**
1. Prep the Ingredients - Wash and dry potatoes. Slice cornichons. Slice pickled onion if using.
2. Boil the Potatoes - In a pot, boil potatoes in salted water for 15-18 minutes until tender. Drain and keep warm.
3. Melt the Cheese - Heat raclette grill or broiler. Place cheese slices in individual pans or on heatproof dish. Melt for 2-3 minutes until bubbly and golden.
4. Prepare Accompaniments - Arrange cured meat, cornichons, and pickled onions on serving plates.
5. Serve - Place warm potatoes on plates. Pour melted raclette over potatoes. Add pepper and salt to taste.
6. Enjoy - Serve immediately with accompaniments and cold beer.

**Recipe 3: Aargau Cheese & Onion Tart (Chäs-Wähe)**
Flaky pastry meets creamy cheese and caramelized onions. A hearty Swiss favorite, ready fast.

**the breakdown**
- prep time: 15 min
- cook time: 25-30 min
- serves: 4
- total: ~45 min

**ingredients:**
- 1 sheet puff pastry (thawed)
- 6 oz Gruyère cheese (grated)
- 2 medium onions (thinly sliced)
- 3 eggs
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 tbsp butter
- Fresh thyme (chopped)
- Nutmeg (pinch)
- Salt & pepper to taste
- Olive oil

**Note/Tip:** Let tart cool slightly before slicing. Crispy edges are the best part.

**instructions:**
1. Prep the Ingredients - Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Thinly slice onions. Grate cheese. Chop thyme.
2. Caramelize Onions - Heat butter and oil in pan over medium heat. Add onions and pinch of salt. Cook 12-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden and soft.
3. Prepare Custard - In bowl, whisk together eggs, cream, nutmeg, salt, and pepper until smooth.
4. Assemble Tart - Roll out puff pastry on baking sheet. Spread caramelized onions evenly over pastry, leaving 1-inch border.
5. Add Cheese & Custard - Sprinkle grated Gruyère over onions. Pour egg mixture over top. Fold edges of pastry slightly inward.
6. Bake - Bake for 25-30 minutes until pastry is golden and custard is set. Garnish with fresh thyme.
7. Serve - Let cool 5 minutes. Slice into squares or wedges. Serve warm or at room temperature.
