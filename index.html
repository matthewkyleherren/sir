Easy Chicken Tandoori
Bold, oven-charred chicken in just 20 minutes. Marinated in rich Hungry Tiger flavor.
the breakdown
prep time
10 min
cook time
10 min
serves
2
total
~20 min
ingredients
10 oz Chicken Thigh, cut into 1-inch pieces
1 Lime (zested and halved)
Scallions (thinly sliced)
½ jar Hungry Tiger Tikka Masala Sauce
Fresh Cilantro (for garnish)
Marinate the chicken for extra depth of flavor. Charred edges = oven magic.
instructions
1
Preheat the oven

Preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C).

2
Prepare ingredients

Thinly slice the scallions. Zest the lime and cut it in half.

3
Marinate the chicken

Toss the chicken with Hungry Tiger sauce and marinate for 30 min.

4
Prepare Baking Sheet

Lightly oil a baking sheet.

5
Bake Chicken

Arrange the chicken on the baking sheet. Bake 7–10 minutes, rotating halfway, until cooked and slightly charred.

6
Plate and Garnish

Plate the chicken with lime halves. Garnish with lime zest, scallions, and cilantro.

7
Enjoy!

Squeeze lime over before eating.

Chicken Tikka Masala with Rice
A comforting classic with tender chicken and rice. Simmered in bold, creamy masala sauce.
the breakdown
prep time
10 min
cook time
20 min
serves
2
total
30 min
ingredients
8 oz chopped chicken breast or thigh
1⁄2 cup rice
3⁄4 cup water
1 small onion (use half)
1 green pepper (optional for extra flavor/crunch)
1⁄2 jar Hungry Tiger Tikka Masala Sauce
Fresh cilantro (roughly chopped)
1 tbsp butter
Salt & pepper to taste
Cooking oil
Add green chili for heat or lime for brightness. Great over rice, naan, or even solo.
instructions
1
Prep the Ingredients
  • Wash and dry all produce.
  • Peel and halve the onion; thinly slice one half (use whole onion if doubling recipe).
  • Roughly chop the cilantro.
  • Pat the chicken dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper.
2
Cook the Rice

In a small pot, combine:

  • 1⁄2 cup rice
  • 3⁄4 cup water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Bring to a boil.

Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15–18 minutes, until rice is tender.

Remove from heat and keep covered until ready to serve.

3
Cook the Chicken, Onion, and Green Pepper
  • Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add sliced onion and a pinch of salt. Cook until onion is slightly softened (about 2–3 minutes).
  • Add chopped green pepper and cook for 1–2 minutes until slightly softened.
  • Add seasoned chicken to the pan.
  • Cook until browned all over and fully cooked (5–8 minutes, until internal temp reaches 165°F/74°C).
4
Add the Sauce
  • Stir in half a jar of Hungry Tiger Tikka Masala Sauce.
  • Cook while stirring for about 2 minutes, until fragrant.
  • Reduce heat to medium-low and let simmer for 1–2 minutes, until sauce thickens and chicken is fully cooked. (If sauce is too thick, add a splash or two of water.)
  • Stir in half of the chopped cilantro.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Turn off heat.
5
Finish the Rice
  • Fluff the cooked rice with a fork.
  • Stir in 1 tbsp butter.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
6
Serve
  • Divide the buttered rice between plates or shallow bowls.
  • Top with chicken tikka masala.
  • Garnish with remaining cilantro.
  • Optionally, sprinkle a pinch of green chili for heat.

Paneer Tikka Masala with Rice
Soft paneer meets fragrant masala and buttered rice. A cozy vegetarian favorite, ready fast.
the breakdown
prep time
10 min
cook time
18-20 min
serves
2
total
~ 30 min
ingredients
8 oz paneer, chopped into bite-sized pieces
1⁄2 cup rice
3⁄4 cup water
1 small onion (use half)
1 green pepper (optional for extra flavor/crunch)
1⁄2 jar Hungry Tiger Tikka Masala Sauce
Fresh cilantro (roughly chopped)
1 tbsp butter
Salt & pepper to taste
Cooking oil
Stir paneer gently to keep it from crumbling. Pairs beautifully with buttered rice.
instructions
1
Prep the Ingredients
  • Wash and dry all produce.
  • Peel and halve the onion; thinly slice one half (use whole onion if doubling recipe).
  • Roughly chop the cilantro.
  • Chop paneer into 1/4 inch cubes.
2
Cook the Rice

In a small pot, combine:

  • 1⁄2 cup rice
  • 3⁄4 cup water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Bring to a boil.

Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15–18 minutes, until rice istender.

Remove from heat and keep covered until ready to serve.

3
Cook the Paneer, Onion, and Green Pepper
  • Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add sliced onion and a pinch of salt. Cook until onion is slightly softened (about 2–3 minutes).
  • Add chopped green pepper and cook for 1–2 minutes until slightly softened.
  • Add paneer to the pan.
  • Cook for 1–2 minutes until paneer is lightly golden and heated through, stirring gently to avoid breaking.
4
Add the Sauce
  • Stir in half a jar of Hungry Tiger Tikka Masala Sauce.
  • Cook while stirring for about 2 minutes, until fragrant.
  • Reduce heat to medium-low and let simmer for 1–2 minutes, until sauce thickens and paneer is fully coated. (If sauce is too thick, add a splash or two of water.)
  • Stir in half of the chopped cilantro.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Turn off heat.
5
Finish the Rice
  • Fluff the cooked rice with a fork.
  • Stir in 1 tbsp butter.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
6
Serve
  • Divide the buttered rice between plates or shallow bowls.
  • Top with paneer masala.
  • Garnish with remaining cilantro.
  • Optionally, sprinkle a pinch of green chili for heat.
